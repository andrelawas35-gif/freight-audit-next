Lesson 2: The Core Flow Stages

In this lesson, we will break down the journey of a single piece of freight data as it moves through the six stages of our post-shipment pipeline.

Stage 1: Ingestion

This is where data enters the platform. We ingest data from five canonical sources:

  • Carrier APIs: JSON carrier invoices
  • EDI 210: Electronic Data Interchange transactions
  • WMS CSV: Client Warehouse Management System exports (Shipments)
  • 3PL APIs: Fulfillment and storage feeds
  • SFTP Polling: Automated drops of carrier files

Every ingestion event creates an Ingestion Batch (tracking row counts and status) and leaves raw records untouched.

Stage 2: Normalization

The platform must protect its downstream systems from carrier-specific quirks. Normalization acts as a shield.

Source-specific adapters take the raw payloads and transform them into a canonical format using functions like stageInvoice() and stageShipment().

A key part of this stage is Code Mapping. If we encounter a carrier surcharge code we haven't seen before, an exception is created. The Data Clerk AI proposes a mapping to our platform standard codes. A human analyst confirms the suggestion, and the mapping becomes Learned.

Stage 3: The Audit Engine

Once data is cleanly staged, the operational audit engine (Parcel or 3PL) gets to work. It processes jobs from a Postgres-backed audit_jobs queue.

The engine evaluates the shipments and invoices against the Rulebook. Rules are evaluated in strict precedence order: Contract rules > Carrier rules > Global rules.

If a violation (like an overcharge or invalid accessorial) is found, the engine produces an Audit Result (also called a Finding).

Stages 4, 5, and 6: Findings, Disputes, and Recovery

4. Findings Queue: Audit Results are queued up for Staff Review. Staff can bulk-file or dismiss these findings.

5. Disputes: Findings that are filed become Disputes. This is a formal challenge to the carrier. We use AI (Response Parser) to read carrier responses and update the dispute state (e.g., won, dismissed, partial, appealed).

6. Recovery: Successful disputes result in recovered cash for the client. But the journey doesn't end there! These findings are tagged with Behavioral Tags (e.g., PREVENTABLE_BY_GATEWAY) to feed into the pre-shipment Readiness Assessment, turning today's loss into tomorrow's prevention.
