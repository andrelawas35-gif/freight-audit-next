Lesson 1: High-Level System Design (The Ecosystem View)

Welcome to the Freight Audit Platform! Today, we're zooming out to look at the entire ecosystem. Understanding this flow is crucial before diving into the code.

The Core Flow

The product fundamentally starts as a post-shipment billing audit platform, but it is evolving into a pre-shipment compliance gateway. Here is the architecture of how data moves:

Key Concepts to Understand

1. The Two Engines

There is a critical distinction in the platform between what recovers money today and what prevents loss tomorrow.

  • Audit Engine (Operational): The legacy engines (Parcel and 3PL) that run post-shipment. They look for carrier overcharges based on the rulebook and produce Billing Findings. This drives cash recovery.
  • Policy Evaluator (Strategic): The new evaluator that reads structured policy_rules. It runs in either 'backtest' mode (post-shipment) or 'pre_shipment' mode. It drives readiness assessments and will power the future Gateway.

2. Normalization is the Shield

The audit engine never touches raw carrier payloads. The step between INGESTION and the AUDIT ENGINE is NORMALIZATION. Source-specific adapters transform raw payloads into canonical staging rows. Code mapping resolves carrier-specific codes to platform codes.

3. AI is Suggest-Only

This is a core invariant of the system. While AI is used for parsing disputes and as a data clerk for mapping codes, it only proposes. A human must always confirm before a mapping is learned or a rule is applied.

Knowledge Check

What is the primary difference between the Audit Engine and the Policy Evaluator?
The Audit Engine uses AI to automatically file disputes, while the Policy Evaluator requires human review.
The Audit Engine is operational and finds overcharges post-shipment, while the Policy Evaluator is strategic and powers pre-shipment prevention.
The Audit Engine handles 3PL fulfillment, while the Policy Evaluator handles carrier API ingestion.
